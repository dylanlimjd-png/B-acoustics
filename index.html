Reading the numbers — a quick key
\n
\n
\n
NRC0 — 1
\n
Noise Reduction Coefficient
\n

How much sound a surface soaks up instead of reflecting. Higher means a softer, less echoey room.

\n
\n
\n
STChigher = quieter
\n
Sound Transmission Class
\n

How well a wall or floor blocks sound passing through it. The higher the rating, the quieter the room next door.

\n
\n
\n
dB−10 ≈ ½ as loud
\n
Decibel
\n

The unit of loudness. A drop of about 10 dB sounds roughly half as loud to the human ear.

\n
\n
\n
RT60seconds
\n
Reverberation time
\n

How long a sound lingers before fading away. Shorter times keep speech crisp and intelligible.

05 / Expertise
\n

Quiet is engineered, not decorated.

\n

Acoustic comfort — soundproofing, sound isolation and noise reduction — is a measurable physical property, not a finish added at the end. We pair acoustic engineering with hands-on architectural detailing, working to the same standards, instruments and field methods used on major civic and commercial projects in Singapore.

\n
\n
\n
Standards we work to
\n
    \n
  • — ISO 3382 · room acoustics
    • \n
  • — ISO 16283 · field insulation
    • \n
  • — NEA boundary noise limits
    • \n
  • — BCA & Green Mark
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
Calibrated measurement
\n

Class 1 sound-level meters, impulse-response capture and tapping-machine testing. Every claim we make is backed by data taken on site, not estimated.

\n
\n
\n
Modelled, then verified
\n

We simulate the room before construction and measure it after. Projects close out against an agreed number — we sign off on data, not opinions.
